The University of North Carolina Wilmington (UNCW) Center for Marine Science (CMS) is hiring a Research Computing and Data Professional to support their growing coastal and marine research computing environment.

CMS is an internationally recognized research center supporting a wide range of computationally intensive research areas, including genomics and bioinformatics, oceanographic modeling, natural products chemistry, marine geology, aquaculture, and environmental science.

**About the Role**

- Support and enable research use of CMS's HPC environment, including software installation, application porting, troubleshooting, and scheduler support (Slurm).
- Partner with researchers, staff, and students to optimize computational workflows and improve research productivity.
- Collaborate with UNCW ITS on HPC operations and infrastructure.
- Develop and deliver training in HPC, scientific programming, and research computing best practices.
- Mentor student researchers and HPC interns.
- Contribute to grant proposals and support computational and data-intensive research projects.

**What They're Looking For**

- Experience working in Linux-based research computing environments.
- Proficiency in Python or R and experience with software development practices such as Git/GitHub.
- Experience with job schedulers such as Slurm or LSF.
- Knowledge of parallel computing and scientific workflows.
- Strong communication and collaboration skills with the ability to support researchers from diverse disciplines.

**Preferred Qualifications**

- PhD in a computational or natural sciences discipline.
- Experience supporting shared HPC resources and training researchers.
- Experience with GPU computing, container technologies (Docker/Apptainer), or Linux system administration.
- Demonstrated ability to work independently while collaborating effectively with faculty, students, and university stakeholders.

Apply [here](https://jobs.uncw.edu/postings/40169). Feel free to reach out to the CMS team directly with any questions about the position, the research computing environment, or UNCW in general.
